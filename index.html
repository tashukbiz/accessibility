What I do

WCAG 2.2 AA audits

Structured evaluations against the WCAG 2.2 Level AA success criteria, using the W3C’s WCAG-EM method, so nothing important is missed.

Keyboard & screen-reader testing

Manual testing with the keyboard alone and with screen readers (VoiceOver, NVDA), because most real barriers only show up when you use the page like this.

Fixes, not just faults

A front-end background means every issue comes with a recommendation a developer can use — often the exact markup or pattern to reach for.

Why this matters to me

I started in code, writing HTML, and moved into UX/UI design because how something feels to use matters as much as whether it works. That is what I care about most.

I believe the web was built for everyone, and everyone deserves equal access to it — the same ability to find, understand, and act on what they need. Today, too many people can’t use the internet comfortably, and I don’t accept that as the way it has to be. We are all different, but we share an equal right to receive information and to take part. That belief is what moved me into accessibility: I want to help teams build web services that every person can reach, understand, and use on equal terms.
