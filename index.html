What I do

WCAG 2.2 AA audits

Structured evaluations against the WCAG 2.2 Level AA success criteria, using the W3C’s WCAG-EM method, so nothing important is missed.

Keyboard & screen-reader testing

Manual testing with the keyboard alone and with screen readers (VoiceOver, NVDA), because most real barriers only show up when you use the page like this.

Fixes, not just faults

A front-end background means every issue comes with a recommendation a developer can use — often the exact markup or pattern to reach for.

Why this matters to me

A government jobs site, a bank, a booking form — for many people with a disability, a single broken control is the difference between doing something themselves and not being able to at all. I came to accessibility from design and front-end development, and I like that the work is both technical and human: you fix the code, and a real person can get on with their day.
